Schooling and Grades

I recently completed my Masters of Applied Science at the University of British Columbia, specializing in applied machine learning within Mechanical Engineering. Before that, I earned a Bachelor of Applied Science in Engineering Physics, along with a year of Electrical Engineering studies.

During my time at UBC, I served as the student representative for my Engineering Physics cohort in 3rd-year, 4th-year, and for graduation (5th-year). This role involved lots of organizing and collaboration, and I found it both rewarding and enjoyable!

My academic background spans complex mathematics, software development, physics, Electrical Engineering, firmware, and Mechanical Engineering. On this page, you'll find details about many of the courses I've taken (though not all). While grades aren't the sole measure of ability, I believe my strong performance across these subjects highlights my capacity to tackle challenging topics.
